Brand foundations for a well-renowned in-memory database.

What's Redis?

Redis is an open-source, in-memory data structure store that can be used as a database, cache, and message broker. It was created by Salvatore Sanfilippo (antirez) in 2009 and has since become one of the most popular and widely used NoSQL databases.

My role

As a brand designer, I created various logo directions to convey Redis concept and define an identity. After some rounds of polishing and refining I chose 3 logo options to present in a logo contest organized by antirez. One of those options was the winner and today, it still remains as the official Redis logotype.

Concept

Unlike traditional relational databases, Redis offers built-in data types such as lists, hashes and strings to store related data. Similar to how a kids fit shape game organizes various shapes to fit into corresponding holes, Redis data types allow for a quick and simple organization of different types of data.

The Redis logo incorporates the shape fitment metaphor to capture the versatility and playful nature of Redis, while also incorporating the classic disc stack shape to represent its function as a database.

Anatomy

The Redis logo is composed by two main components, the Redis emblem on the left, which is an isometric representation of the Redis brand concept, and the wordmark on the right, to communicate the brand name.

The typography is a rounded sans-serif font, which reinforces the playful and friendliness nature of Redis. The color palette is predominantly composed of shades of red as primary color, followed by gray and white as secondaries, making the Redis logo different from other databases logos, which usually use blue.

Conclusion

Designing the Redis logo was a fun and rewarding challenge. Back then, I had very limited knowledge on databases and how they work but this project game a lot of insights, specially on how data is structured. Special shout-out to Michel Martens who was kind enough to educate me and also for the collaboration brainstorming the logo concept.

After 13 years of the logo release, I’m really grateful and proud for being part of the Redis story (in a tiny way of course). As of today, the logo and Redis brand remains unchanged and it appears that they have aged quite well.
