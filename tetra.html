Tetra Insights

Maximizing the value and reach of user research & qualitative data

Who’s Tetra Insights?

Tetra Insights is an end-to-end user research platform that streamlines the qualitative research process. From customer interviews to insight analysis, Tetra allows teams and organizations to easily collaborate and make data-driven decisions. The platform offers transcription, annotation, analysis tools and much more, to help organizations gain a deeper understanding of their customers to build and offer better products.

My role

As part of the webuild team, I helped transform the existing Tetra insights app into an usable and scalable user research platform that facilitates the collection, analysis, organization, and sharing of research insights in a people-friendly way.

The Challenge

Tetra was struggling with their existing UX. It was time-consuming, fragmented, and inefficient, leading to challenges in managing and organizing research studies, recruiting participants, analyzing data, and collaborating with colleagues. Users needed a comprehensive and streamlined platform that simplifies the research process, improves efficiency.

Solution

We designed and developed a comprehensive platform that addresses specific user needs such as providing a user-friendly interface to manage, organize and share insights from research studies, scheduling and participating in user interviews and more. We also designed new design language in combination with a scalable and modular design system.

Discovery

During kick off and initial interview calls with Tetra stakeholders, we quickly realized where we would need to focus our research efforts. We ultimately based our research in the following areas:

1.

Analyze and audit the existing UX and UI of the Tetra platform and all the workflows.

2.

Qualitative interviews with users to understand their pain points, needs, and goals.

3.

Competitors analysis of research platforms similar to Tetra, to identify opportunities.

4.

Async surveys to gather quantitative data on user behaviors and preferences.

Insights

Research was crucial to take informed decisions later on. By understanding user needs and goals, we were able to design a platform that meets and solves specific needs in a streamlined and efficient user experience. We categorized our learnings in the following buckets:

Analysis
  • A simple way to organize data throughout the research process.
  • Researchers need to be able to easily visualize and analyze their data and generate insights.
  • Need to collaborate with colleagues, share insights, and work together to sinthesize data.
Interviews
  • Participants often struggle with scheduling and the logistics of participating in research studies.
  • Researchers need a way to easily recruit participants for their studies and schedule interviews.
Platform
  • Privacy and security are major concerns for both researchers and participants.
  • Should allow to save time and increase efficiency for researchers, Main focus on insights and analysis rather than the logistics of the research process.

Concept

Unlike traditional relational databases, Redis offers built-in data types such as lists, hashes and strings to store related data. Similar to how a kids fit shape game organizes various shapes to fit into corresponding holes, Redis data types allow for a quick and simple organization of different types of data.

The Redis logo incorporates the shape fitment metaphor to capture the versatility and playful nature of Redis, while also incorporating the classic disc stack shape to represent its function as a database.

Anatomy

The Redis logo is composed by two main components, the Redis emblem on the left, which is an isometric representation of the Redis brand concept, and the wordmark on the right, to communicate the brand name.

The typography is a rounded sans-serif font, which reinforces the playful and friendliness nature of Redis. The color palette is predominantly composed of shades of red as primary color, followed by gray and white as secondaries, making the Redis logo different from other databases logos, which usually use blue.
